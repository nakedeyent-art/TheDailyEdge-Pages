Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

The Daily Edge is designed as a bring-your-own-provider analytics app. Users may enter their own API keys, broker credentials, watchlists, preferences, and local account information. The first public launch keeps these credentials on the user's device.

Data Stored On Device

  • Local app account records and optional two-factor setup.
  • User-supplied provider API keys.
  • User-supplied broker or execution credentials.
  • Watchlists, favorites, settings, cached scans, and paper-tracking results.

Data Sent To Providers

When a user enters a provider key and refreshes a market, the app may send requests directly to that provider, such as The Odds API, Alpha Vantage, Twelve Data, Brave Search, AI providers, OANDA, Polymarket, Robinhood Crypto, or Coinbase, depending on what the user configured.

Each provider handles data under its own privacy policy and terms.

What Daily Edge Does Not Collect At Launch

  • Daily Edge-owned copies of user provider keys.
  • Daily Edge-owned copies of broker private keys.
  • Payment card numbers; Apple handles subscription billing.
  • Sportsbook login credentials.
  • Real-money sports bets.

Account And Data Deletion

Users can use Settings > Reset App Data to delete local API keys, broker credentials, accounts, preferences, cached scans, watchlists, and saved app data from the device.

Tracking

The first public launch does not use third-party tracking for advertising. If tracking is added later, this policy and App Store privacy answers must be updated first.

Contact

For support, use the App Store support link for The Daily Edge or visit Support.
